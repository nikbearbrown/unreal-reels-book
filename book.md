# Book pitch — Unreal Reels: A Practitioner's Guide

*Author-facing. Not for readers. The clearest statement of what this book is before a word of it is drafted.*

## The argument

A finished book is not the end of the work — it is raw material for a dozen other forms. The same chapter that reads well on a page can become a ten-minute narrated lecture, a one-minute explainer, a mini-biography, or a kids' language-learning music video. **Unreal Reels is the pipeline that makes those videos from books, and this book teaches you to run it.**

The thesis in one line: **audio is the master clock, the human signs every gate, and the machine does everything in between.** A reader who finishes this book can take a chapter they wrote and hand back a watchable, accurate, narrated video — not by prompting "make me a video" and accepting slop, but by driving a phase-gated pipeline where quality is controlled at cheap decision points.

## The gap it fills

Single-prompt video tools produce generic, subtly-wrong output because nothing anchors them. Unreal Reels is different in one decisive way: **it works downstream of a real, fact-checked book.** The narration expands the chapter's own prose; the figures are the chapter's own figures. This book is the missing manual for that difference — how to get from `chapter.md` to `lecture.mp4` without losing the book's authority along the way.

## The reader

A practitioner — a teacher, author, or course-builder — who has a book (their own, or an AI+1 book) and wants video from it. Comfortable pasting commands an agent hands them; not necessarily a programmer. Every chapter ends with an artifact they made.

## Shape

Twelve chapters in three parts: **Foundations** (the two laws, the beat sheet, the storyboard engine), **Making videos** (Songbird, Bios, Explainer, and the chapter-to-lecture pipeline), and **Scale & trust** (silent mode, fidelity checking, the design system, the AI+1 ecosystem, and making a lecture your own). Problem-first, whole-task: every chapter opens with a short overview of why the system works the way it does, then exercises that produce a real artifact.
