# Risks — Unreal Reels: A Practitioner's Guide

## Comparable texts

Most adjacent material is prompt-cookbook "AI video" content built around a single monolithic model. The nearest structural cousins are film-production handbooks and this author's own practitioner guides (`ai-for-teachers`, `ai-for-graphs`). None teach an *audited, book-anchored* video pipeline — that is the open lane.

## Who it's for, sharpened

Teachers, authors, course-builders with material to convert. Not programmers; they drive an agent. The book must never assume a terminal-native reader — the agent does the typing (an inherited AI+1 principle).

## Feature priorities

1. The two laws and the beat sheet (everything rests on them).
2. The chapter → lecture pipeline (the highest-value path for the education audience).
3. Silent mode + fidelity (the "150 books" scale story).
4. The reels aspects (Songbird/Bios/Explainer) — valuable but more service-dependent.

## Out of scope

- Deep coverage of any one external service's UI (Higgsfield/FLUX/Seedance move fast — teach the seams, reference the services).
- Video editing craft beyond the pipeline (color grading, sound design).
- The internals of AI+1 book generation (its own book; referenced here).

## Top adoption risks + mitigations

- **Service drift.** The paid-service steps age fastest. *Mitigation:* teach the pipeline seams and the free/local path (SVG figures, local TTS, audiogram) as first-class, so the book stays useful even as services change.
- **"Just prompt it" skepticism.** Readers may expect one-prompt magic. *Mitigation:* Chapter 1 confronts slop-vs-spine head-on; the fidelity chapter shows why anchoring to a book matters.
- **Accuracy anxiety.** Educators fear the video misstating their material. *Mitigation:* the fidelity chapter and the fact-commons grounding check are load-bearing, not an afterthought.
