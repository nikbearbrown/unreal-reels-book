# Vision — Unreal Reels: A Practitioner's Guide

## Positioning

The practitioner's manual for the Unreal Reels video pipeline — a companion to the AI+1 book-generation system. Where AI+1 teaches you to make a book, this teaches you to make video *from* that book: lectures, explainers, mini-bios, music videos. It sits beside the author's other practitioner guides (`ai-for-teachers`, `ai-for-graphs`) in register and structure: problem-first, exercise-driven, an artifact per chapter.

## Learner profile

A teacher, author, or course-builder who already has written material and wants it in video form. They can talk to a CLI agent (Cowork, Claude Code, Codex) and paste commands the agent hands them, but they are not necessarily programmers. They care about two things above all: that the video is **accurate** (it doesn't misstate their book) and that it quickly becomes **theirs** (not generic AI output).

## Central argument

Good educational video does not come from a single prompt. It comes from a pipeline with two non-negotiable laws — **audio is the master clock** and **the human signs every gate** — and a machine that does the rest. Anchored to a real book, the pipeline produces a *spine*: a high-quality, accurate starting point an expert refines into something special, rather than slop they discard.

## Comparable texts and the field

Most "AI video" material teaches prompting a monolithic model. This book teaches an *audited pipeline* instead — closer in spirit to a film-production handbook than a prompt cookbook. Its distinctive claim is fidelity-by-construction: the video is downstream of a fact-checked book, so accuracy is a fidelity check, not a fact-check from scratch.

## What success looks like

A reader finishes with a narrated lecture video they built from one of their own chapters, a mental model of the phase gates, and the ability to run silent mode over a whole book — knowing exactly which decisions only they can make.
